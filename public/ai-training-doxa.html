Six Sessions at a Glance

Each session builds on the last. Half teaching, half building. Every session ends with a deliverable.

Session 1 · The Roadmap

Find AI opportunities your business actually has.

You know AI is a big deal. You also know that "do AI" is not a strategy.

  • Scan four offices: Revenue, Talent, Innovation, Operations
  • Sort opportunities by leverage and difficulty
  • Write a categorized AI roadmap with a clear first project

Outcome You stop chasing every shiny demo. You leave with three projects to ship and a reason for each.

Session 2 · Packaged Prompts

From prompts to packaged AI.

You re-type the same brief into ChatGPT every week. Your team does too. Nobody compounds.

  • Convert ad-hoc prompts into named, reusable assets
  • Add inputs, examples, and guardrails so the team can run them
  • Build your first packaged prompt live, end-to-end

Outcome Anyone on the team can run "draft launch announcement" or "score inbound lead" without re-explaining the brief.

Session 3 · Wire the Workflow

Move beyond a single prompt into a full workflow.

A single great prompt is not a workflow. The work is what happens between the prompts.

  • Map your idea using the 7-step Trigger Sequence
  • Define what AI handles vs. where a human stays in the loop
  • Wire the trigger, the action, the routing, the review

Outcome A messy 3-day process collapses into a 30-minute loop. The human only sees what needs a human.

Session 4 · Decision Logic

Teach your workflow how to decide.

An HR director wants AI to screen inbound job applications. But not make final decisions.

  • Define classification logic: auto-advance, flag for review, decline
  • Set routing rules by role type and experience level
  • Build response templates for each path

Outcome AI executes the decision logic you designed. The team only reviews the top 20%, without missing a single qualified candidate.

Session 5 · The Agent

Set the guardrails so AI can act alone.

A marketing CEO wants to stop manually following up with webinar attendees who didn't convert.

  • Build an agent that triggers when a lead goes cold
  • It classifies intent, selects the right message, sends it
  • Set the boundaries of what the agent is never allowed to do

Outcome Re-engages 200 cold leads per month automatically. A human is only looped in when someone replies.

Session 6 · Production

The brief you hand to a developer.

You've built something that works. Now you need your tech team or a contractor to make it permanent.

  • Translate your prototype into a clear written spec
  • Define what you lead vs. where an engineer takes over
  • Walk out understanding the developer superpower: The Stack

Outcome You stop being the bottleneck between a prototype and a shipped product. The handoff is clean.
