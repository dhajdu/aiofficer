03
The Gap

Passable is enough for personal tasks.

It is not enough for programs. When you are leading an AI program, passable is not enough. You are producing work that changes how your team operates, how your company makes decisions, and how leadership invests in AI. Generic output does not change anything.

04

RACE + CRA

First pass gets the content. Second pass meets the standard.

05
Framework

RACE gets you 70%.

R
Role
Who is the AI acting as?
A
Action
What should it do?
C
Context
What's the situation?
E
Example
What does good look like?
06
Framework

CRA: The quality lock.

C — Constraints
What are the limits? Budget, time, brand rules, scope.
R — Requirements
What must be included? The non-negotiables.
A — Acceptance Criteria
How do you know it is good enough? Specific pass-fail checks.
07
Example

RACE vs. RACE + CRA

RACE only:
Decent. Could be about any energy drink. Generic tone. Includes irrelevant competitors.
RACE + CRA:
Dana could walk into a meeting with this. Specific. Focused. Ready to send.
08

The AI Interview

The gap between what you would have written and what AI asks. That gap is the value.

09
Technique

Stop guessing what AI needs.

Without it:
You guess what AI needs. Output is generic. Could have come from anyone.
With it:
AI finds gaps you didn't know you had. Output is yours.
10
Example

Live example: Customer profiles

You: "I need to build customer profiles for BOLT. Before you start, ask me every question you need answered."

AI asks about current vs. aspirational customers, pricing, distribution, what has failed before. Those questions reveal what you didn't know was missing.

12

On-Demand Learning

You do not memorize frameworks. You access them.

13
Technique

The habit that compounds.

Ask AI: "What framework should I use for this?"

Apply it. Learn it. Keep it.

You learn the framework and get the deliverable at the same time. This skill compounds every session.

14

VRC

Creating output is only half the job.

15
Framework

Value. Risk. Checkpoint.

V — Value
Where does the benefit come from? Is the payoff clear?
R — Risk
What are the top two ways this fails?
C — Checkpoint
What is the early signal it is working?
16
Scaling

VRC works at every scale.

Same three questions that evaluate a competitor analysis also evaluate an entire AI initiative before it gets budget and headcount. VRC is a career skill, not just a prompting technique.
