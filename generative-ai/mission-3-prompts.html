Mission 3 — Challenge Prompts

Every prompt is copy-paste ready. Replace anything in [brackets] with your specific information. Use these with any AI tool — ChatGPT, Claude, or Gemini.

💡 How to use these: Copy any prompt, paste it into your AI tool, fill in the brackets with your real context. The more specific you are in the brackets, the better the output. Generic answers get generic results. Don't write prompts. Buddy it.
RACE + CRA: Two-Pass System
Pass 1 — RACE
Stack Your Frameworks
Use RACE to draft your first version. Gets you 70% of the way there.
You are a [role relevant to this task]. I need you to [specific action]. Here is the context: [full situation, relevant data, audience, constraints]. Here is an example of what good looks like: [paste an example or describe the format, tone, and structure].

Produce a first draft.
Pass 2 — CRA
Tighten With CRA
Apply CRA on top of your RACE draft to lock the quality. Gets you to something your boss can use.
Now tighten this draft using CRA.

Constraints: [specific limits - length, scope, budget, brand rules, things to avoid]
Requirements: [non-negotiables that must appear - specific data points, key messages, required sections]
Acceptance Criteria: [how will I know this is good enough? Give me specific pass-fail checks]

Revise the draft to meet all three criteria. Show me only the revised version.
The AI Interview - Master Prompt
Framework Prompt
The AI Interview
Before starting any important deliverable, let AI ask you questions first. Finds gaps in your thinking you didn't know existed.
I need to produce [describe the deliverable]. This will be reviewed by [who will see it] and needs to [what it must accomplish].

Before you write anything, ask me every question you need answered first. I want you to understand my situation, my audience, and my constraints before you produce any output. Ask at least 5 questions. Make them specific.
Learn a Framework On Demand
Framework Prompt
On-Demand Framework Learning
Ask AI to teach you the right framework for your problem, then apply it immediately to your situation.
I need to [describe the business problem or deliverable you are working on].

Recommend the best framework for this type of work. Explain the framework simply so I understand how it works. Then apply it directly to my situation using the information I have provided.

After you apply it, suggest two alternative frameworks I could have used instead and explain when each one is better suited than the one you chose.
Competitor Analysis: RACE + CRA
Pass 1 — RACE
Build Competitor Analysis
Use RACE to create a detailed competitor analysis for your product or service.
You are a market analyst specializing in [industry]. I need a competitor analysis for [product/service], a [description] targeting [target audience].

Use only the data I provide. Do not make up competitors, pricing, or market share numbers.

For the RACE draft: identify the top [number] direct competitors in the [price range / market segment], compare their positioning, pricing, and distribution, and highlight one weakness per competitor that [product] can exploit.

Keep the total analysis under [word count].
Pass 2 — CRA
Tighten Competitor Analysis
Apply CRA to make the competitor analysis focused and leadership-ready.
Now apply CRA to tighten this analysis.

Constraints: [limit to specific competitors, exclude certain segments, maximum length]
Requirements: [must include pricing, must include distribution channel, must include one specific weakness]
Acceptance Criteria: [describe what a good competitor entry looks like - e.g., "I should be able to read any entry and immediately know what we do differently"]
Customer Profile: AI Interview Approach
Framework Prompt
Build Customer Profiles
Let AI interview you before creating customer profiles. Gets specific profiles tied to your actual business.
I need to build customer profiles for [product/service]. These profiles will be used for [purpose - e.g., a go-to-market brief, a leadership presentation, a sales team deck].

Before you write anything, ask me every question you need answered. I want you to understand our current customers, our aspirational customers, our pricing, our distribution, and any past experiments that failed.

Ask at least 6 questions. Make them specific to the information a product launch team would actually need.
Brand Positioning on Demand
Framework Prompt
Generate Brand Positioning
Ask AI to teach you the right brand positioning framework, then apply it immediately to your product.
I need to develop brand positioning for [product/service]. I am not sure which framework to use.

Recommend the best brand positioning framework for a [type of product/market]. Explain the framework simply so I understand how it works. Then apply it directly to [product] using the data I have provided.

After you apply it, suggest two alternative frameworks I could have used and explain when each one works better.

Use only the information I have provided. Do not invent claims or statistics.
VRC Evaluation
Evaluation Prompt
Check Before You Send
Run VRC before any output goes to your manager, a client, or a leadership meeting. Value, Risk, Checkpoint.
I am going to paste my output below. I need you to evaluate it using the VRC framework before I send it.

V - Value: Where does the benefit come from? Is the payoff clear and specific? Who gets value from this and how?

R - Risk: What are the top two ways this output fails or misleads? Be honest. I need to know before my manager sees this.

C - Checkpoint: What is the earliest real-world signal that this output is actually working?

After your evaluation, give me a clear recommendation: send it as-is, fix specific things (list them), or start over. Explain why.

Here is the output: [paste your output]
Go-to-Market Brief Assembly
Final Project
Assemble Your Brief
Combine all your sections into a single, coherent seven-section go-to-market brief.
I have completed the following sections for a go-to-market brief: [list which sections you have]. I am going to paste them below.

Assemble them into a single, coherent go-to-market brief with these sections:
1. Executive Summary (brief overview - one page or less)
2. Market Opportunity (the case for this product)
3. Competitor Analysis
4. Customer Profiles
5. Brand Positioning
6. Risks (VRC applied at scale)
7. Next Steps (what leadership needs to decide)

Make sure the sections flow together logically. The executive summary should be a standalone summary of the full brief, not just an introduction. Flag any gaps or inconsistencies between sections.

VRC at Scale: AI Initiative Review
Evaluation Prompt
Evaluate an AI Program
Use VRC to evaluate an entire AI initiative before it gets budget or headcount.
I want to evaluate an AI initiative I am planning using the VRC framework at the program level.

The initiative: [describe the AI program - what it is designed to do, who uses it, what data it requires]

V - Value: Where does the ROI come from? How much time, money, or quality does this program improve, and can that be measured?
R - Risk: What are the top two ways this entire program fails before it delivers value? Think about data quality, adoption, technical complexity, and governance.
C - Checkpoint: What is the earliest signal - in the first 30 days - that this program is on track before I am too far in to change direction?

Give me a complete VRC evaluation and tell me whether I should proceed, revise, or reconsider the scope.
